Featured

Ghana · Nigeria · International

El Anatsui

Working from his studio in Nsukka, Nigeria, El Anatsui transforms thousands of discarded bottle caps and aluminum scraps into vast, shimmering tapestries — works that hang like cloth but speak like history. His art asks what we throw away, and what endures.

Born 1944, Anyako, Ghana Sculpture · Installation

Collage

Kenya · USA

Wangechi Mutu

Mutu's collages weave together bodies, nature, and technology into surreal new forms — interrogating how African women are seen, and reimagining what they can become.

Born 1972, Nairobi Collage · Sculpture
Painting

DR Congo

Chéri Samba

Kinshasa's most celebrated painter fills large canvases with vivid colour and handwritten text, turning everyday Congolese life into pointed social commentary.

Born 1956, Kinto M'Vuila Painting
Textile

Malawi · South Africa

Billie Zangewa

Zangewa hand-stitches raw silk into intimate self-portraits — scenes of domestic life in Johannesburg that quietly assert the power and complexity of Black womanhood.

Born 1973, Blantyre Silk tapestry
