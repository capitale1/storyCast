Why we exist

African art belongs to everyone who wants to see it.

StoryCast was built on the belief that access to art should not depend on where you live, whether you can visit a gallery, or whether you can see. We publish African art and artists with full image descriptions, artist statements in plain language, and every accessibility feature we know how to build.

What we publish

Art in every form

We work with artists, estates, curators, and cultural institutions across the continent to document and share African creative work.

🎨

Painting & Drawing

From Kinshasa's popular painters to the abstract canvases coming out of contemporary Lagos, Dakar, and Addis Ababa — every work accompanied by a full visual description.

🗿

Sculpture & Installation

Three-dimensional works presented through multiple views with written descriptions that convey scale, material, texture, and spatial relationship.

🧵

Textile & Craft

Kente weaving, imigongo, kanga, mud cloth, silk tapestry — traditions that carry encyclopaedias of cultural knowledge in their patterns and making.

📷

Photography

Documentary and fine art photography from across the continent, presented with extended captions and full contextual descriptions for every image.

WCAG AA compliance

Accessibility commitment

We follow WCAG 2.1 Level AA throughout. Here is what that means on StoryCast.

Image Accessibility

  • Every artwork has a figcaption describing medium, dimensions, and visual content in plain language.
  • Extended image description panels on every artist page give a detailed written account of each work for screen reader users.
  • Decorative SVGs and background images are hidden from assistive technology using aria-hidden.
  • All meaningful images have descriptive alt text that conveys the content and purpose of the image, not just its file name.

Navigation & Structure

  • A skip link is the first focusable element on every page, letting keyboard users jump straight to the main content.
  • Every page uses clearly labelled landmark regions so screen reader users can jump between header, navigation, main content, sidebar, and footer.
  • All interactive elements are reachable and usable by keyboard alone — no mouse required.
  • Visible focus rings appear on every link, button, and form field when navigating by keyboard.

Colour & Readability

  • Body text achieves a contrast ratio of over 14:1 against the page background — more than three times the WCAG AA minimum.
  • Colour is never the only way information is communicated — labels, shapes, and text always accompany colour-coded elements.
  • If your device is set to reduce motion, all animations and transitions on StoryCast are automatically turned off.
  • Text can be zoomed to 200% without content overlapping or disappearing.

Screen Reader Support

  • Collapsible panels (description sections, mobile navigation) use aria-expanded so screen readers announce whether each panel is open or closed.
  • The active page is identified in every navigation menu with aria-current so screen reader users always know where they are.
  • Artist metadata (birthplace, medium, dates) uses definition list markup so screen readers associate each label with its value correctly.
  • Icon-only elements and ambiguous controls carry aria-label descriptions so screen readers can explain what they do.

Get in touch: If you encounter any barrier while using StoryCast — whether you cannot access an image description, a page is not working with your screen reader, or anything else — please contact us at access@storycast.example. We treat accessibility reports as urgent and aim to respond within 48 hours.
