Why we exist

African art belongs to everyone who wants to see it.

StoryCast was built on the belief that access to art should not depend on where you live, whether you can visit a gallery, or whether you can see. We publish African art and artists with full image descriptions, artist statements in plain language, and every accessibility feature we know how to build.

What we publish

Art in every form

We work with artists, estates, curators, and cultural institutions across the continent to document and share African creative work.

🎨

Painting & Drawing

From Kinshasa's popular painters to the abstract canvases coming out of contemporary Lagos, Dakar, and Addis Ababa — every work accompanied by a full visual description.

🗿

Sculpture & Installation

Three-dimensional works presented through multiple views with written descriptions that convey scale, material, texture, and spatial relationship.

🧵

Textile & Craft

Kente weaving, imigongo, kanga, mud cloth, silk tapestry — traditions that carry encyclopaedias of cultural knowledge in their patterns and making.

📷

Photography

Documentary and fine art photography from across the continent, presented with extended captions and full contextual descriptions for every image.
